RESEARCH INTERESTS: Statistical ecology, spatio-temporal models, population ecology and dynamics, causal networks, interface of graph theory and phylogenetic trees

CURRENT RESEARCH:
  • Improved statistical models for movement of Alaskan groundfish in relation to environmental changes and long-term climate conditions (supported by a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, 2015-2019)
  • Applying novel statistical approaches to modeling the effect of climate changes on population dynamics of reindeer, which are of considerable cultural and socio-economic importance to the native Sami people of the region and the Nordic countries they inhabit
  • Collaborating with US and African graduate students and researchers to model local population dynamics of elephants in Kenya using age-structured population models with environmental variables such as drought indices
CURRENT POSITION: From May 2018 - Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Biological Sciences, Auburn University
